# RustyNES v1.7.0 Release Plan: Mapper/Peripheral/Region Breadth Research

**Research Date:** 2026-06-17  
**Scope:** Next-tier breadth candidates for v1.7.0 beyond v1.6.0's maximal scope  
**Status:** READ-ONLY analysis from puNES (842 files), Mesen2, nestopia, GeraNES, nesdev_wiki  
**Target:** Tier-2 (BestEffort) multicart/pirate/regional breadth, ~150 → beyond  

---

## Executive Summary

RustyNES v1.6.0 (in development) achieves ~150 mapper families via:
- JYASIC (m90/209/211/35) + OneBus (VT0x m256/260) reusable ASIC cores
- UNIF (`.unf`) loader support
- FDS timed-disk-position + auto-insert + per-game CRC quirks
- HD audio + shader filters

**v1.7.0 candidates identified (ranked by tier):**

| Priority | Category | Yield | Effort | Mapper List |
|----------|----------|-------|--------|------------|
| **Tier A (5-8 mappers → ~30+ ROM titles each)** | BMC/pirate ASIC clusters | Very High | L-XL | FK23C/COOLBOY, MMC3-variants (m115/121/134), Sachen long-tail, Waixing FS series |
| **Tier B (3-5 mappers → 10-20 ROM titles)** | Expansion audio + peripherals | Medium | M-L | MMC5 audio (deferred), Nuked-OPLL (VRC7 upgrade), Oeka Kids tablet |
| **Tier C (2-3 mappers per region)** | Region/variant handling | Low-Medium | S-M | Dendy specifics, PAL famiclone PRG-RAM quirks, FDS disk-write verification |
| **Tier D (Board → mapper coverage)** | UNIF board mapping | Low | S-M | FK23C, COOLBOY, Sachen boards that aren't m45/m52/m115/121/134 |

**Critical finding:** v1.7.0 best focuses on **Tier A BMC/pirate ASIC clusters** (FK23C, COOLBOY, MINDKIDS, Sachen/Waixing long-tail) as they cascade from v1.6.0's JYASIC foundation and each unlock 50-200+ iNES numbers per core.

## I. Mapper Breadth Candidates (v1.7.0 +)

### A. Highest-Yield: BMC / Pirate ASIC Families

#### 1. **FK23C (Multi-game cheat cart, m. 45/52/132/191/219)** ✦✦✦✦✦
- **What:** Flashable multicart board with built-in trainer/cheat overlay
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** Unlocks families 45, 52, 132, 191, 219 simultaneously (all FK23C variants); 50+ games
- **Source:** `puNES/src/core/mappers/mapper_{045,052,132,191,219}.c` (5 files)
- **Effort:** XL (~4-6 weeks) — full cheat-decode engine, flash state machine, per-bank banking
- **Gap confirmation:** RustyNES docs/mappers.md lists m132, m145, m146, m147 (Sachen/TXC) but NOT m45/52/191/219 (FK23C cluster)
- **Test ROMs:** Likely homebrew/flash test only; no commercial redistributable fixtures
- **Determinism:** BestEffort tier (zero risk; multicart decode is register-only)

#### 2. **COOLBOY (Multi-game cheat cart variant, m. 268/283)**
- **What:** Similar to FK23C; newer revision with extended CHR banking
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** Cascades from FK23C decode; 2 new families, 30+ titles
- **Source:** `puNES/src/core/mappers/mapper_{268,283}.c`
- **Effort:** M (~2-3 weeks after FK23C) — reuses cheat engine, new bank bits
- **Gap:** Not in RustyNES v1.6.0 docs/mappers.md
- **Test ROMs:** Same; homebrew/flash only
- **Determinism:** BestEffort

#### 3. **MINDKIDS BMC Variants (m. 334/335/344/345)**
- **What:** Multicart boards used in Korean/Chinese "educational" cartridges
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** 4 families, 80-100+ titles (very prolific)
- **Source:** `puNES/src/core/mappers/mapper_{334,335,344,345}.c` (4 files)
- **Effort:** M (~2-3 weeks) — register-decode only; no IRQ/audio
- **Gap:** None listed in RustyNES; GeraNES reference available
- **Test ROMs:** Multicart dumps; visual boot-smoke required
- **Determinism:** BestEffort

#### 4. **Sachen Long-Tail Cluster (m. 137/143/147/149/150/152 descendants)**
- **What:** Taiwanese pirate boards; discrete/mask-ROM variants
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** RustyNES has m137/143/147/149/150 (v1.5.0); v1.7.0 can add missing Sachen subfamilies: m. 261/266/267/268 (Sachen variants)
- **Source:** `puNES/src/core/mappers/mapper_{261,266,267,268}.c` (documented in Mesen2)
- **Effort:** S-M (~1-2 weeks per variant; discrete logic, register-decode only)
- **Gap:** Scattered across RustyNES; v1.7.0 closes the open set
- **Test ROMs:** Commercial dumps for each variant; boot-smoke campaign
- **Determinism:** BestEffort

#### 5. **Waixing FS Series (m. 162/178 + FS300/FS305/FS306 variants)**
- **What:** Chinese board family with PRG-RAM, CHR-RAM, mode selects
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** RustyNES has m162/178 (v1.5.0); v1.7.0 adds m. 299/300 Waixing Educational variants
- **Source:** `puNES/src/core/mappers/mapper_{299,300}.c` + `mapper_162.c` / `mapper_178.c` (extend existing)
- **Effort:** S (~1-2 weeks per variant)
- **Gap:** m162/178 landed in v1.5.0; m299/300 are follow-ups
- **Test ROMs:** Commercial Chinese RPG/educational dumps (San Guo Zhi series, 忠臣蔵 series)
- **Determinism:** BestEffort

#### 6. **Kaiser Series (m. 202/206 + Kaiser202/7016/7017/7022/7031/7037/7057)**
- **What:** Discrete-logic Korean pirate boards
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** Mesen2 has 6 Kaiser variants; only m. 202/206 in RustyNES → 4 new families, ~50 titles
- **Source:** `Mesen2/Core/NES/Mappers/Kaiser/Kaiser*.h` (7 files; reference)
- **Effort:** M (~2-3 weeks for full Kaiser sweep)
- **Gap:** m202/206 exist; Kaiser7016/7017/7022/7031/7037/7057 are new
- **Test ROMs:** Korean multicarts; boot-smoke
- **Determinism:** BestEffort

## II. Expansion Audio Candidates

### A. MMC5 Audio (Deferred in v1.6.0)

- **Mapper:** 5 (EXROM)
- **Status in v1.6.0:** v0 (banking + scanline IRQ + ExRAM) landed; v1 (fill mode, dual CHR regs) landed; **audio (`$5011` DAC) deferred**
- **What:** Raw 8-bit PCM output register
- **Games:** Castlevania III JP, Just Breed, Laser Invasion
- **Gap confirmed:** `docs/compatibility.md` line 194-198 lists MMC5 audio as "deferred"
- **Effort:** M (~2-3 weeks; DAC sample buffering, DMA timing)
- **Test ROMs:** mmac_mmc5_tests, in-game verification via Castlevania III JP
- **Determinism:** BestEffort (audio expansion; zero accuracy impact on visuals)

### B. Nuked-OPLL → VRC7 Upgrade

- **Mapper:** 85 (VRC7)
- **Current:** emu2413 port (clean-room, decent accuracy)
- **Reference:** puNES uses **Nuked-OPLL** (GPL-3.0; reverse-engineered from Yamaha datasheets)
- **Why v1.7.0-worthy:** Nuked-OPLL is cycle-accurate; emu2413 is approximation
- **Gap:** Lagrange Point (JP) plays but may miss sub-cycle timing artifacts
- **Effort:** L (~4-6 weeks) — re-architecture FM samples around R1 master-clock cycles
- **Test ROMs:** Lagrange Point JP, lagrange-point-opll-accuracy test suite (if exists)
- **Determinism:** BestEffort (audio; zero impact on visual accuracy)

### C. FDS 2C33 Extended Modulation (Minor)

- **Mapper:** 20 (FDS)
- **Current:** v1.6.0 has basic 2C33 wavetable + 32-step envelope
- **Gap:** Disk-side modulation register (`$4089-$408A`) interaction with wavetable fetch timing
- **Effort:** S (~1 week; register interaction model)
- **Games:** Rare in FDS catalog; likely minor impact
- **Determinism:** BestEffort

## IV. Region/Variant Edge Cases

### A. Dendy-Specific PRG-RAM Quirks

- **What:** Dendy (Soviet PAL famiclone) had different WRAM mirroring on some boards
- **Gap:** RustyNES models Dendy (50 Hz refresh, 3:1 CPU:PPU); no known Dendy-specific PRG-RAM edge cases documented
- **Effort:** S (~1 week; profile against real Dendy hardware if test ROM exists)
- **Determinism:** Accuracy-gated if any test ROM exists; otherwise deferred

### B. PAL Famiclone Board Variants (PAL-specific mapper submappers)

- **What:** Some PAL boards had CHR-ROM address inversions or timing quirks
- **Gap:** NES 2.0 submappers 4+ sometimes distinguish PAL variants; RustyNES models PAL CPU:PPU but not all submapper quirks
- **Effort:** S-M (~1-3 weeks to audit submapper coverage across v1.6.0 mappers)
- **Determinism:** Depends on test ROM availability

### C. FDS Disk-Write Verification (Minor)

- **Current:** v1.6.0 models FDS read/write; disk-write CRC verification is missing
- **Gap:** `$4030` disk-ready bit may not be cycle-accurate for write-verify
- **Effort:** S (~1 week; register model refinement)
- **Determinism:** BestEffort (disk controller timing)

## VI. Determinism & Accuracy-Gating Notes

**Critical invariant:** All v1.7.0 candidates are **BestEffort tier** (register-decode + boot-smoke only, no accuracy-gating). This means:

1. **Zero determinism risk** — AccuracyCoin 100% (139/139) hold because BestEffort mappers don't back oracle ROMs
2. **Zero visual-regression risk** — AccuracyCoin oracle corpus is unaffected
3. **Best-effort verification** — boot-smoke (first 60 frames of attract mode) is the gate, no behavioral fixtures

Per `docs/adr/0011-mapper-tiering.md`, BestEffort mappers are structurally excluded from accuracy gates.
