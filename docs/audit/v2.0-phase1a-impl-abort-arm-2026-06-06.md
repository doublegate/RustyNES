- **It is NOT 16 aborts:** with the `mc-r1-dmc-abort-probe`, only **3** aborts

This is a bounded abort-occurrence-model problem, not a cycle-accounting rewrite.

## Phase 1A — arm-at-`$4015`-enable (TriCNES model)

TriCNES (`Emulator.cs` `_6502`) evaluates the implicit abort **at the `$4015`
reload-enable**, reading the live DMC byte-timer: fire when
`APU_ChannelTimer_DMC == 10` (get) / `== 8` (put). Implemented behind
`mc-r1-impl-abort-at-enable` in `apu.rs::write_status`, setting
`pending_dmc_abort` (consumed by the `mc-r1-dmc-abort-cancel` read1/write1 path:
1-cycle halt on a get / silent cancel on a write — the latter is the Loop2 `$520`
"no abort on the JSR push cycle" behaviour).

### The arm condition is SOLVED

Diagnostic counters (`impl_arm_enable_seen` / `impl_arm_fired`) + the per-`$4015`
evlog establish, at the 12 implicit reload-enables (all `bytes_remaining==0`):

```
timer @ enable:  96 19 12 24 19 4 26 22 2 18 10 8
```

- `timer` passes through **10 and 8** at exactly the **last two** reload-enables
  (the idx-10,11 positions) and at **no other** implicit enable.
- `timer ∈ {10,8}` (parity-agnostic) → `impl_arm_fired == 4` across the 3 loops
  — arms at precisely the right iterations. (The parity-specific `10→get / 8→put`
  gave 0 fires: the two enables sit on the opposite parity to TriCNES's
  convention — a separate, minor calibration, not the blocker.)

So `timer*2`/cuo was a **misapplied** mapping here (it belongs to the *explicit*
cuo path); the raw `dmc.timer` is directly comparable to TriCNES's `10`/`8`.

### The residual: the abort has nothing to truncate

Despite `impl_arm_fired == 4`, the `$500` sweep stays `00`×16. Root cause:

- At these enables `bytes_remaining == 0` — the 1-byte sample has **already
  finished**, so there is **no reload DMA in flight**. Setting `pending_dmc_abort`
  then makes the read1/write1 path inject an **out-of-band 1-cycle halt**, which
  the re-syncing `CalculateDMADuration` loop (it `DMASync`s every iteration)
  **absorbs uniformly** → no per-iteration Y change.
- The real hardware implicit abort **truncates the measured end-of-sample reload
  DMA itself** to 1 cycle. Our DMC output unit never **emits** that end-of-sample
  reload-fetch request, so there is nothing for the `$4015`-enable race to abort.

## The genuine residual (next work)

Port Mesen's `DeltaModulationChannel::Run()` last-bit → `StartDmcTransfer`
behaviour so the DMC output unit **emits a reload-fetch DMA request at the end of
the sample** (even non-looping), cycle-accurately on the byte-timer. Then the
`$4015`-enable race aborts **that** request (1-cycle DMA at idx 10,11), and the
arm condition already proven here (`timer ∈ {10,8}`) fires it. This is the DMC
output-unit model (plan Phase 1/Phase 3), **not** the full CPU rewrite, and
**not** the abort arm-threshold.

## Post-script (same session): the abort sweep is GATED on `CheckDMATiming Y==4`

After the arm was solved I attempted to truncate the *measured looping reload*
DMA (latch `dmc_abort_next_reload`, consumed in `dmc_step_reload_arm`) so the
`$500` sweep would read `01` at the armed iterations. It fired (truncated 2
reloads) but the sweep stayed `00`×16 — because **`CheckDMATiming Y` is still 3,
not 4**, on this branch in *both* budget states. `TEST_Implicit/ExplicitDMAAbort`
open with `JSR CheckDMATiming / CPY #4 / BNE FAIL`, so the **entire abort cluster
is gated on Y==4 first** — the abort-sweep work (arm + truncation) is *downstream*
of, and moot until, the reload-span fix.

`CheckDMATiming` = `CycleClockBegin / NOP / NOP / CycleClockEnd` — it times the 2
NOPs while a **looping reload** DMC DMA steals cycles between them; Y = the reload
DMA's cycle cost. Hardware/answer-key wants a reload = **4 cycles** (halt-on-put +
the extra align); our floor path (`bus.rs::dmc_dma_step_impl`, the
`not(mc-r1-dmc-mesen-span)` branch) yields a uniform **3** → Y=3 → the `CPY #4`
precondition fails for the whole cluster.

**Corrected residual ordering (make-or-break first):**
1. **Reload-DMA span 3→4** (`CheckDMATiming Y`) — the documented "missing align
   cycle"; the principled fix is the Mesen `_needHalt`/`_needDummyRead` two-cycle
   preamble so a *reload* halts on a put cycle (4) distinct from a *load* halting
   on a get (3). The `mc-r1-dmc-mesen-span` scaffold exists; its 1-cycle variants
   (`-span-collapse`/`-trailing-stall`/`-getparity-flip`) are all refuted. This is
   plan **Phase 1**, the make-or-break, and is upstream of everything below.
2. **Then** the implicit-abort arm (SOLVED here, `timer ∈ {10,8}`) + the
   end-of-sample reload-fetch emit + truncation will register on the now-Y==4 base.

So the Phase-1A arm is correct and banked, but the **first** thing the next
session must close is the reload-span 3→4 (Phase 1 DMC-DMA scheduler), not the
abort sweep.

## Synthesis — the reload-span gate IS the C1-vs-DMA coupling (rewrite needed *here*)

Tested `mc-r1-reload-span4` (parity-conditional 1-cycle defer of the *reload* arm
only, to flip its first-halt cycle get→put → span 4). Both parities fail:
- defer when `!put_cycle` → **inert**, Y stays 3 (arm-time `put_cycle` does not map
  to the first-`dmc_dma_step` start parity 1:1 under the F-2 end-of-cycle tick).
- defer when `put_cycle` → **breaks the looping reload entirely** (no GET ever
  fires → `DMASync_50CyclesRemaining` spins forever → the whole DMA cluster goes
  `(unset)`).

This reconciles this session's results with the prior `RW-2` conclusion into one
architectural fact — **the reload-span 3→4 and the C1 IRQ trio are coupled**:
- `CheckDMATiming`, `DMASync`, and the abort-sweep `CalculateDMADuration` *all*
  measure/anchor on the **same looping reload DMA**. Its span/phase therefore
  cannot be locally corrected — a reload-only defer is either inert or starves the
  GET that `DMASync` re-anchors on.
- Making the reload genuinely 4 cycles requires the **unified per-cycle counter
  with OAM-DMA folded in** (`RW-2`) so the get/put parity the reload halts on is
  coherent across OAM + DMC + CPU.
- But `RW-2` (OAM on the unified counter) **regresses the C1 IRQ trio**
  (`cpu_interrupts_v2/4-irq_and_dma`, 92.09%→89.21%) unless the IRQ φ2 sampling is
  *also* done coherently on that same counter.

So closing the DMA tail and keeping C1 is the **same** problem — exactly the
"C1-vs-DMA conflict" the goal names. The bounded part (Phase 0 + the abort ARM) is
done and proves a *lever* won't do it. The remaining part genuinely needs the
**Stage-D coherent scheduler**: each OAM cycle wrapped `start_cycle →
unified_dma_step → end_cycle` so it samples IRQ/NMI via the *same* φ2 `_prev*`
pipeline (the surface `RW-2` bypassed), DMC-get-before-OAM-get priority, parity
from the one counter. That is the `mc-r1-full-cpu` Stage-D rewrite scoped in
`~/.claude/plans/toasty-noodling-scroll.md` (Phase 1B) — a multi-session structural
change, the single remaining path, and now precisely justified (not assumed) by the
inert/starve dichotomy above.

## Trace finding (per-cycle, mc-r1-full-cpu): the span is ALREADY 4 — Y=3 is GET fire-phase

`trace_dma_4015 $0478` over the Implicit-Abort window (irq-timing-trace + mc-r1-full-cpu)
shows every DMC DMA is **span 4**, not 3:

```
cyc 48393618  R  $FF0E   (last CPU read before halt)
cyc 48393619  r  $FF0F   in_dmc=1 put=1   halt
cyc 48393620  r  $FF0F   in_dmc=1 put=0   align
cyc 48393621  r  $FF0F   in_dmc=1 put=1   align
cyc 48393622  r  $FFC0=$00  in_dmc=0 put=0   GET (sample $00)
cyc 48393623  R  $FF0F   (CPU resumes)
```

Run-length census of the whole window: **92 spans of 3 halt cycles + GET = uniform
span 4**, every span starting on `put=1`. So under the Stage-D coherent base the
reload-DMA span is correct (4); `CheckDMATiming Y=3` is **NOT a span-length bug**.

**Reframe (corrects the 20+ "span 3→4" attempts):** `CheckDMATiming`/the abort
sweep measure via `CalculateDMADuration`, which counts the GET's open-bus-`$00`
position by sliding 1 cycle/iteration — i.e. Y is the GET **fire-phase** relative
to the clockslide anchor. Our span-4 DMA's GET lands 1 cycle off the answer-key
phase. But that GET is the SAME looping-reload GET `DMASync_50CyclesRemaining`
locks onto, so any fire-phase shift is either absorbed (Y is relative to the
DMASync-anchored cadence) or starves DMASync — the exact inert/wedge dichotomy
seen across arm-defer, prealign, and the 17+ prior levers. This is the
evidence-grounded form of the "DMC reload-DMA span/fire-phase" residual: not a
missing align cycle (the span is 4), but a DMASync-coupled 1-cycle fire-phase that
cannot be locally corrected. Closing it requires the reload's fire-phase to match
hardware *intrinsically* (the output-unit empty→reload-request timing), measured
against the clockslide anchor — the C1-vs-DMA core, on the now-C1-safe Stage-D base.
