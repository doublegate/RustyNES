## TL;DR

**Neither target closes via the halt-subpos approach.** Both require the
**deferred cycle-exact DMC-DMA-on-master-clock span model** — a fundamentally
different axis than the reload-arm / abort-scheduling sub-position that closed
Implicit. Rigorously pinned via the `scan_dma_abort` zero-page sweeps + a new
default-off diagnostic lever (`mc-r1-dmc-abort-subpos`). **Zero regression:**
Implicit stays closed, AccuracyCoin held at **94.24% / 8**, SH* 6/6, nestest
ROM golden 0-diff, floor (umbrella-off) byte-identical. Nothing was forced into
the umbrella (ADR-0002).

## Method recap

The three abort tests all gate on `JSR CheckDMATiming; CPY #4` then run an
answer-key sweep. The earlier "error 2 = Y-gate" reading was refined: **error 2
means the test PASSED the CheckDMATiming Y=4 gate and failed the post-gate
answer-key sweep** (the `INC <ErrorCode` runs after the gate). So, exactly like
Implicit's `$540`, the residual is a few off-by-one entries in a measured-
duration sweep — but on a DIFFERENT code path than the reload arm.

The sweeps live in zero-page (`$50-$5F` Explicit Loop1; `$50-$6F` DMC+OAM
Loop1+Loop2), captured in `scan_dma_abort` with a previous-frame snapshot to
avoid cross-test contamination.

## Explicit DMA Abort — the abort-truncated span is structurally 1 cycle short

```
got = [04 04 04 04 04 04 03 03 00 01 01 00 00 00 00 00]
key = [04 04 04 04 04 04 03 04 01 01 00 00 00 00 00 00]
diff                      ^7 ^8       ^10
```

Indices 0-6 match (full 4-cycle DMA, then the first abort clip to 03 at X=6).
The failure is the **non-monotonic recovery at X=7**: hardware's abort write
just MISSES the DMA window so it completes full (`04`), then X=8/9 hit the GET
(`01,01`). RustyNES collapses X=7 to `03` and is short by one through X=8/10.

A new diagnostic lever `mc-r1-dmc-abort-subpos` (env `RUSTYNES_ABORT_SUBPOS`)
shifts `cycles_until_output` in `schedule_explicit_dmc_abort_if_needed` by a
swept CPU-cycle delta. Sweep `[-2..+2]`:

| `ABORT_SUBPOS` | Explicit `$50-$5F` |
|---:|:---|
| -2 | `04×6 03 03 00 00 00 01 01 00 00 00` |
| -1 | `04×6 03 03 00 01 00 00 00 00 00 00` |
|  0 | `04×6 03 03 00 01 01 00 00 00 00 00` |
| +1 | `04×6 03 03 00 00 00 00 00 00 00 00` |
| +2 | `04×6 03 03 01 00 00 00 00 00 00 00` |

The sweep moves which iteration aborts (the re-enable tail, `armed` 3→2→1), and
Implicit `$540` stays Key3 throughout — but **index [7] is IMMOVABLE at `03`**
(key wants `04`). The required non-monotonic `...03,04,01...` recovery cannot be
synthesized by shifting the abort SCHEDULING point: it is a property of the
abort-truncated DMA SPAN, which is structurally 3 cycles where hardware places a
4-cycle span at that sub-phase. Also inert to the reload-arm `RUSTYNES_SUBPOS_DELAY`
(the Implicit lever fires exactly 2× and never on Explicit).

**Axis: cycle-exact abort-DMA-span model.** When the explicit `$4015`=#0 cancel
lands on a specific sub-cycle of the DMC DMA, the resulting span must be modeled
at 4-cycle (halt/dummy/align/get) resolution with the correct cancel placement.
The current Option-C `cancel_dmc_dma` collapses this.

## DMC DMA + OAM DMA — the OAM-overlap interaction is not modeled at all

Clean (previous-frame-snapshot) capture:

```
got $50-$5F = [03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04]
key $50-$5F = [04 03 04 03 04 03 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 01]
got $60-$6F = [03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 02 03]
key $60-$6F = [02 01 02 01 02 00 01 02 03 03 04 03 04 03 04 03]
```

RustyNES produces a flat `03,04` alternation (the normal 3/4-cycle DMC DMA on
alternating get/put parity). Hardware produces `04,03` (opposite start phase)
AND, critically, a deep `02,01` section in the middle — the regime the asm
describes where the DMC DMA halt coincides with the OAM DMA so "the DMC DMA
appears to only take 2 cycles" (and then 1). **RustyNES never emits `02` or
`01`** anywhere in the sweep: it does NOT model the DMC-DMA-during-OAM-DMA
priority interaction (DMC takes priority on get cycles, OAM on put cycles, plus
the OAM realignment cycle after a DMC get — nesdev `DMA#DMC_DMA_during_OAM_DMA`).
Inert to both `RUSTYNES_SUBPOS_DELAY` and `RUSTYNES_ABORT_SUBPOS`.

**Axis: DMC-DMA-during-OAM-DMA interaction model** (a constituent of the
cycle-exact DMC-DMA-on-master-clock span work). This is wholly absent, not an
off-by-one — the entire get/put-priority + alignment-cycle interleave between
the two DMA engines must be implemented.

## Why these differ from Implicit (which DID close)

Implicit's failure was a clean reload-arm boundary: the byte-timer's APU-rate
(2-CPU-cycle) quantization could not express a 1-CPU-cycle arm delay, and a
per-cycle countdown at the `cannot_run==2` exclusion supplied exactly the
missing half-step. Explicit and DMC+OAM fail INSIDE the DMA span structure
(abort truncation; OAM overlap), which no scheduling-point lever can reshape —
they need the span itself modeled cycle-exactly. This matches the long-standing
project position that the ~17 DMC/DMA residuals gate on the single deferred
cycle-exact DMC-DMA-on-master-clock axis.

## Recommendation

Defer Explicit DMA Abort + DMC DMA + OAM DMA to the cycle-exact
DMC-DMA-on-master-clock span/interaction rewrite (the same axis as the ~15
remaining DMC/DMA/SH*-adjacent residuals). The `mc-r1-dmc-abort-subpos` lever is
retained default-off as a validated diagnostic that pins the Explicit axis.
