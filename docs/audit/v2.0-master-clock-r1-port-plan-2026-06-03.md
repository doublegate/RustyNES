## The architecture (validated by both research passes)

main/pivot's DMA works because **the DMC byte-timer, the DMA span, and the CPU cycle share ONE
integer clock** (`tick_one_cpu_cycle_lockstep`): the variable 3↔4-cycle DMC span feeds back into
the cycle parity and **dynamically breaks the DMASync mod-lock** (`DMASync_TheGoodOne`: period 432,
exit exactly 406 cycles to the next DMA; `DMA+OpenBus` Test 2 is deterministic, cycle-exact). A
*constant* clock shift cannot break a mod-lock; only the *dynamic* span feedback can.

R1's regression on the branch came from **S1 (`mc-apu-subcycle`)** + **S2 (`r4-cpu-dma`)** +
**`mc-dmc-rdy-stall`** — moving the APU/DMC tick off the once-per-`cpu_clock` cadence and routing
DMA through `Cpu::process_pending_dma`, which **decoupled the DMC timer from the CPU cycle**
(measured: the DMC arm lands on `FD7x` PRG fetches, not `$4000`). The coordinated-core-plan proved
the DMA *code* is not the cause (legacy `drain_dma` and `r4-cpu-dma` fail the identical 16 tests) —
the cause is the **APU-tick order / clock decoupling**.

**Separability (the crux):** R1's exact-mc PPU catch-up (needed for on-time `$2002`/NMI READ timing
and the `end_cycle` T_last-1 IRQ sample) can coexist with main's bus-side DMA **iff** the DMA span
and the APU/DMC tick stay on the integer CPU-cycle clock. The d5c7ddf "DMA-coherence" layer
(`take_dma_mc_consumed` / `dma_tick_one_cycle`) exists precisely to keep `master_clock` and
`ppu_clock` advancing together across a bus-side DMA span (documented byte-identical/inert).

## F-2 reconciliation attempts (2026-06-03) — the APU-phase tension is IRRECONCILABLE by phase choice

User-authorized "take on F-2." Two grounded, pivot-specific levers tried on top of Phases 1-4
(both reverted; neither shippable). The wedge is `NMI Overlap IRQ` → `JSR DMASync_50CyclesRemaining`
spinning because the DMC `$4000` open-bus conflict never lands at the cycle DMASync expects.

**Attempt 1 — drain placement (NEUTRAL).** main drains the DMC/OAM DMA in `cpu_read`/`cpu_write`
(WITH the held bus address); my Phase-3 `cpu_clock` added a `drain_dma(None)` (before `bus.read`,
losing the held `$4000`). Removed it so DMA is serviced only in `bus.read`/`bus.write` (= main's
placement). Result: **IDENTICAL 98.80%/83 wedge** — the failure is NOT where the DMA is drained.

**Attempt 2 — APU/DMC tick phase (BREAKS C1).** main ticks the APU/DMC at END of cycle
(`tick_one_cpu_cycle`, after the read); R1's `cpu_clock` ticks it at cycle-START (before the read),
a 1-cycle DMC-fire phase shift. Moved `apu_advance_one` to a new `Bus::cpu_clock_apu` called at
end_cycle (before `handle_interrupts`). Result: **`cpu_interrupts_v2/3-nmi_and_irq` AND `#4-irq_and_dma`
REGRESS** (was passing). ⇒ the APU-at-START tick is LOAD-BEARING for C1 (the φ2 IRQ sample reads the
APU/DMC IRQ line; it must be the start-tick value).

**Conclusion (empirically re-derived the branch's wall):** C1 needs APU-at-START (the IRQ-line
sample), DMASync needs the DMC-fire at main's END phase — **no single APU-tick phase satisfies both.**
The genuine fix must DECOUPLE the DMC byte-timer fire-phase (end, for DMASync) from the APU IRQ-line
sample-phase (start, for C1) — i.e. the branch's T-1 "DMC byte-timer on the integrated per-cycle
counter," which T-2 proved emergent-timing-neutral (the byte-timer free-runs; period fixed by the
rate reg independent of span). This is the cumulative per-fetch DMC-span reconciliation =
`project_r1_dma_regression` / `project_t_track_dmc_integration` — NOT a knob, multi-week, 17+
rollbacks. DO-NOT-RETRY (this pivot): drain-placement (neutral), whole-APU-tick-phase-move (breaks C1).

## F-2 span↔fire feedback (2026-06-03) — REFUTED at the DMC-tick level; needs the interleaved-DMA model

Attempt: restore the span↔DMC-fire feedback by advancing the DMC byte-TIMER during the DMA span
(`tick_dmc_timer_only`, no delay/reload arm — so the variable 3/4 span feeds the next fire-cycle
WITHOUT re-arming a DMA mid-span). Result: **RE-WEDGES** (98.80%/83, NMI Overlap IRQ hangs) — same as
the full free-run. Decisive: **ANY DMC advance during its own DMA span re-wedges** (the rate-$F
1-byte DMC re-fires and cascades into a halt-loop that the `LDA $4000;BNE` poll never escapes);
un-wedging REQUIRES the DMC frozen for the span. So the span↔fire feedback CANNOT be restored by
ticking the DMC during the span — frozen (un-wedged, no feedback, DMA 1/10) and advanced (re-wedged)
are the only two states, and they are mutually exclusive at this level.

⇒ The cumulative-span feedback requires the TriCNES **interleaved-DMA model** (divergence C): one DMA
cycle interleaved per `_6502()` step sharing the per-cycle put-cycle flip-flop, NOT RustyNES's
burst-loop drain at `read1` entry. That is a structural DMA rewrite (`service_dmc_dma` /
`service_dmc_dma_during_oam` / `drain_dma` → per-cycle interleave), the genuine multi-session core —
beyond knob-tuning. Four bounded F-2 levers now refuted (drain-placement, whole-APU-phase, free-run,
timer-only, ext_phase seed). **Best banked: `e43d2be` (un-wedged 83.45%; structural wedge solved;
DMA cycle-exactness = the interleaved-DMA rewrite).**
