## The corrected hardware model (the key finding)

Per **nesdev `DMA.xhtml:325`**: the DMC-DMA open-bus / register conflict "checks bits 4-0 from the 2A03
address bus and bits 15-5 from the 6502 core. **The 6502 core keeps the same address while halted**, so if
it was reading from $4000-401F, the DMA address can unintentionally activate 2A03 registers." Confirmed in:

- **Mesen2** (`NesCpu.cpp:589,676,757-825`): captures `prevReadAddress` (the halted CPU read addr) at DMA
  entry, computes `enableInternalRegReads = (readAddress & 0xFFE0)==0x4000` from it, and `ProcessDmaRead`
  applies the conflict keyed off that captured address — independent of which cycle is the GET.
- **TriCNES** (`Emulator.cs:4132,9250`): `Fetch` gates the conflict on `addressBus` (the latched 6502-core
  address, still $4000), while the register selection uses `Address & 0x1F` (the DMC fetch addr). Conflict
  and GET are the SAME cycle, but the conflict is keyed off the persistent latched address, not the GET.
- **ares** (`fc/cpu/timing.cpp:49-106`): correct span model (does NOT model the conflict — span reference only).

**Therefore on hardware "the conflict lands" ⇔ `(halted_addr & 0xFFE0)==0x4000` (always true when the CPU
was reading $4000), and "span == 4" ⇔ the halt/dummy/align cadence. The two are DECOUPLED.** A correct
model can set the span to 4 by cadence and the conflict still lands because the 6502 core held $4000 across
the whole halt — every halted cycle (halt, dummy, align, get) carries the conflict.

## Why every prior attempt failed (the coupling bug)

In RustyNES's `read1` interleaved model (`cpu.rs:764-790`; `bus.rs:dmc_dma_step_impl`), the `$00` that
DMASync's `LDA $4000;BNE` reads is produced by the bus-access on the DMA cycles, but the *effective*
conflict-landing is tied to the GET's cycle/parity: shifting the span to 4 moves the GET (and the
poisoning) one parity step, off the cycle that aligns with the CPU's `$4000` read — the DMC then "misses"
the read (wedge trace `8cc9c51`: DMC fires at `$EF79` while the CPU reads `$4000`=`$40` unhalted). **No
prior lever changed WHERE the conflict `$00` is produced** — they all shifted the GET/parity/span/timer and
kept the conflict GET-coupled. The untried lever: **produce the conflict `$00` on EVERY halted cycle (keyed
off the captured held `$4000` address, per hardware), so "conflict lands" no longer depends on the GET
parity — then the span can be 4 (correct cadence) without the conflict missing (no wedge).**

## Phase plan (gated; each phase default-OFF behind its own flag; floor = `d09b41a`)

### CM-P0 — VERIFY the coupling (diagnostic, no behavior change)
Confirm the hypothesis before building. Using the wedge-capable `trace_dma_4015` + `irq-timing-trace`,
capture a WORKING (6/10) DMC DMA that halts a `$4000` read and answer precisely:
1. On which cycle(s) of the span is `open_bus`/the returned value driven to the conflict `$00`? Only the
   GET, or also the halt/dummy/align cycles?
2. Does `replay_dma_noop_read(halted_addr)` (`bus.rs:2092`) on the halt cycles actually poison `open_bus`
   to the conflict value, or does it reset it to `$40`? (Agent-3 flagged the trailing re-read RESETS
   open_bus to `$40` — the same mechanism may mean the halt/dummy cycles do NOT carry the conflict.)
3. What does the CPU's real `bus.read($4000)` at `cpu.rs:787` actually return, and from which cycle's state?
**Gate to proceed to CM-P1:** a written determination that the conflict `$00` is currently GET-coupled (if
it's already on every halt cycle, the hypothesis is wrong and we re-plan — do NOT build CM-P1 blind).

### CM-P1 — decouple the conflict: drive it off the held address on every halted cycle
Behind `mc-r1-dmc-held-conflict` (default-off). Port the Mesen/TriCNES model precisely:
- Capture `halted_addr` once at DMA entry (already passed as `addr`); compute
  `enable_internal_reg_reads = (halted_addr & 0xFFE0)==0x4000` once.
- On EVERY DMA cycle (halt, dummy, align, AND get), when `enable_internal_reg_reads`, drive the
  open-bus/register conflict keyed off the held address (`0x4000 | (dmc_addr & 0x1F)` register selection +
  the `$4015`/`$4016`/`$4017` internal-read cases), so the CPU's subsequent `$4000` read sees the conflict
  `$00` regardless of which cycle was the GET. Port `ProcessDmaRead` (`NesCpu.cpp:757-825`) /
  `Fetch`-conflict (`Emulator.cs:9250`) into `dmc_dma_read` + `replay_dma_noop_read`.
- **Critical:** the halt/dummy/align cycles must NOT reset `open_bus` to `$40` (the trailing-stall
  clobber bug, `82536a5`) — they must carry the conflict forward.
**Gate:** the conflict still lands (no wedge) AND it lands on the WORKING 6/10 cases identically (no
regression). Y still 3 at this phase is EXPECTED (we have not changed the span yet).

### CM-P2 — set the span to 4 by cadence (MAKE-OR-BREAK)
With the conflict decoupled (CM-P1), now make the span the hardware-correct 3-or-4 by the load-vs-reload
arm-parity cadence (nesdev `DMA.xhtml:113,115,144-148`: load DMAs 3, reload DMAs 4 unless halt delayed):
- Drive the HALT/DUMMY/ALIGN/GET count from `dmc_need_halt`/`dmc_need_dummy_read` + the arm-time put/get
  parity (`apu.rs:722-755`), NOT from how many loop iterations precede `bus.read`.
- The GET parity can now move freely (the conflict no longer depends on it).
**Make-or-break gate:** `CheckDMATiming` Y 3→4 + the 4 tail tests flip to pass + **6/10 hold + C1 trio
holds + no wedge**. If the span-4 cadence still wedges or breaks the 6/10 even with the conflict decoupled,
that REFUTES the hypothesis and we fall back to RA-4 (ship as-is) — report, do not thrash.
