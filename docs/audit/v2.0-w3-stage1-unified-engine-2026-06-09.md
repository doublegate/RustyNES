## The DMC+OAM informative array (the one PERMITTED structural divergence)

The overlap model is what Stage 1 replaces (floor = sequential nested loops, no
sharing), so the DMC+OAM array was expected to change. It does — and in the
most informative way possible:

```
KEY    $50-$5F = 04 03 04 03 04 03 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 01
KEY    $60-$6F = 02 01 02 01 02 00 01 02 03 03 04 03 04 03 04 03

FLOOR  $50-$5F = 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04   (flat, no sharing)
FLOOR  $60-$6F = 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 03 04 02 03

ENGINE $50-$5F = 03 04 03 04 03 04 01 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 01 02   (02/01 regime APPEARS)
ENGINE $60-$6F = 01 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 01 02 03 04 03 04 02 03
```

The engine's array is the KEY **shifted by exactly one parity position**
(`03,04 | 01,02` vs KEY `04,03 | 02,01`), with the regime transition at the
same idx\[6\] boundary the key has. The shared-cycle 02/01 regime — which the
floor's sequential model can NEVER produce — emerges natively from the one
dispatch table. The single remaining delta is the global floor parity itself,
i.e. precisely the put_cycle end-flip that Stage 2 applies (the
counter-collapse breakthrough parity, where the audits measured DMC+OAM
16/16 == KEY with the Stage-0 boundary fix). This is the strongest possible
confirmation of the two-driver→one-driver thesis: with ONE driver the
"inseparable" Explicit/Implicit-vs-DMC+OAM coupling is gone — Explicit and
Implicit are BYTE-IDENTICAL to the floor while DMC+OAM moved onto the key's
structure.

Result-byte frames shifted by ~1 frame (DMC+OAM 1632→1631, Explicit
1636→1635, Implicit 1645→1644) — the overlap consumes fewer cycles than the
sequential model, so the suite advances slightly sooner. All result codes
identical.

## What was built (the engine)

### One driver, one dispatch (`crates/nes-core/src/bus.rs::unified_dma_cycle_impl`)

A direct port of the TriCNES `_6502` per-cycle DMA dispatch table
(`crates/nes-test-harness/golden/tricnes/tricnes-harness-src/Emulator.cs`
~4233-4357) that standalone DMC, standalone OAM, and the overlap ALL ride:

* **OAM state** (new, `uni_` prefix): `uni_oam_active` (TriCNES `DoOAMDMA`),
  `uni_oam_halt` (`OAMDMA_Halt`), `uni_oam_aligned` (`OAMDMA_Aligned`),
  `uni_oam_addr` (`DMAAddress`). The 513/514 length and the post-GET realign
  are **EMERGENT** — no owed-cycle counter: the halt flag is set only when the
  first serviced cycle lands on the OAM read half; a DMC GET stalls OAM for
  the slot AND forces `uni_oam_aligned = false` so the in-flight byte is
  re-read (TriCNES `DMCDMA_Get` → `OAMDMA_Aligned = false`); the DMA completes
  on the 256th write. DMC state reuses the existing `in_dmc_dma`/`dmc_halt`.
* **OAM activation**: the first serviced cycle after the `$4014` write latches
  the page + the parked CPU address — regardless of any in-flight DMC — which
  natively absorbs the Stage-0 `$4014`-write→first-OAM-cycle gap (lockstep
  `drain_dma` latches OAM before its DMC-pending check).
* **DMC activation**: first serviced cycle latches `in_dmc_dma` + `dmc_halt` +
  the open-bus replay (the floor `dmc_dma_step_impl` latch). A LOAD may not
  enter on the even half (the `mc-r1-dmc-load-get-entry` span-3 rule,
  re-derived at the access point; the pre-cycle form lives in
  `unified_dma_pending`).
* **Dispatch** (one bus slot per cycle): on the DMC get half an unhalted DMC
  GET has priority (fetch + `complete_dmc_dma` + the `$4000` open-bus
  conflict + OAM de-align); otherwise a halted DMC replays the held read's
  side-effect alongside the OAM slot (the lockstep
  `service_dmc_dma_during_oam` noop-body model — the in-tree overlap spec);
  OAM reads on its read half / writes-or-aligns on its write half; halts clear
  at the end of each engine's get half (TriCNES "both halt cycles get cleared
  after a get cycle").

### Floor-parity mapping (what Stage 2 collapses)

The floor's two drivers run on OPPOSITE halves of the shared cycle counter
(`put_cycle == (cycle & 1 == 0)` at the access point):

* DMC GET half = `!put_cycle` (ODD bus cycles) — the emergent
  `dmc_dma_step_impl` span preserved exactly (entry-on-even = span 4,
  entry-on-odd = span 3);
* OAM READ half = `put_cycle` (EVEN bus cycles) — the floor's
  `oam_dma_step` latches 514 when `self.cycle & 1 == 0` and its reads always
  land on even cycles.

The engine expresses BOTH conventions inside the one dispatch (each engine's
halt clears at the end of ITS OWN get half). Stage 2's put_cycle end-flip
merges the two halves onto the references' single get/put labeling — under
which the engine becomes literally the TriCNES table — and the DMC+OAM array
above should snap onto the KEY while the (already-byte-identical) emergent
spans ride the same one driver, with no second driver left to over-grow.

### One loop (`crates/nes-cpu/src/cpu.rs`)

Under the flag, `read1`'s three DMA loops (the standalone DMC drain, the
sequential Stage-D OAM loop, the Program-M overlap loop) and `idle_tick`'s DMC
drain are cfg'd out (not deleted) and replaced by ONE loop:
`while bus.unified_dma_pending() { start_cycle; bus.unified_dma_cycle(addr);
end_cycle }` — every DMA cycle keeps the φ2 IRQ sample (the C1-safe shape).
`write1` keeps only the abort-cancel pre-check. Budget accounting matches the
replaced loops at the floor config (every DMA cycle counts against
`cycles_emitted`; both budget flags are off at floor).

### At floor positions (Stage-1 constraint honored)

Byte-timer / reload-arm / put_cycle all stay at their floor positions
(cycle-start, `apu_phase`-fired): NO nes-apu change at all. The landed
Implicit stack (halt-subpos / abort-cancel / abort-timer-phase /
reenable-phase / load-get-entry) stays composed and functional —
`mc-r1-dmc-bytetimer-end`/`mc-r1-counter-collapse` are NOT composed here.
No parity knob was added anywhere (the DO-NOT-RETRY tables remain binding).

## Scope statement

Stage 1 proves the references' single-driver architecture can carry the floor
byte-for-byte where the floor is correct (Explicit/Implicit/Y/standalone OAM/
standalone DMC) while natively producing the shared-cycle overlap structure
the floor never could. It does NOT move the floor: `mc-r1-dma-unified` stays
default-off and out of the umbrella. Stage 2 (next session) flips the one
engine to the breakthrough parity (put_cycle end-flip composed INTO the
unified dispatch), where the prior coupling-wall (Explicit 04→05 over-grow,
Implicit 02-baseline regress) should vanish because the second driver no
longer exists — the measured DMC+OAM one-parity offset above is the direct
evidence. Save-state serialization of the transient `uni_oam_*` state is
deliberately omitted (states are taken at frame boundaries; cleared on
restore); the budget-flag combos (`mc-r1-dma-cycle-budget`,
`mc-r1-dmc-trailing-stall`) are out of Stage-1 scope and not composed with
the flag.
