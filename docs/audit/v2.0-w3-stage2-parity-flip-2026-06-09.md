## What was implemented (composition route)

`mc-r1-dma-unified-collapse`:

* **nes-core** — composes `mc-r1-dma-unified` + `mc-r1-counter-collapse` (the
  measured apu-side end-flip semantics are reused VERBATIM — no duplication:
  cycle-start `put_cycle = !apu_phase` flip suppressed; `dmc_tick_end` clocks
  the byte-timer + decrements `cannot_run` on `timer_phase = !put_cycle`,
  reload-arms, THEN flips `put_cycle` at the very end; `apu_phase` keeps
  flipping at cycle-start so the C1 φ2-on-master_clock sample is invariant; the
  halt-subpos pre-wrap detector reads the shifted parity — all pre-existing
  `mc-r1-counter-collapse` blocks). Forwards a new **nes-apu raw gate** for the
  three reconciliations below.
* **The merged dispatch** (`crates/nes-core/src/bus.rs::unified_dma_cycle_impl`,
  cfg-split): under the end-flip the access-point `apu.put_cycle()` read IS the
  references' in-cycle `APU_PutCycle` label (TriCNES also flips at end-of-cycle
  — the floor's start-flip was the documented one-position difference). The
  dispatch therefore becomes the literal TriCNES `_6502` table:
  - **GET half** (`!put_cycle`): DMC GET (priority; OAM loses slot + alignment)
    > OAM READ (a halted DMC replays alongside, lockstep noop-body order) >
    halted reads; **BOTH halts clear after a get cycle**.
  - **PUT half**: OAM WRITE/align; a waiting/halted DMC replays the held read.
  - OAM halt latched when the FIRST serviced cycle is a GET (TriCNES
    `FirstCycleOfOAMDMA`).
  - Standalone OAM keeps its absolute parity by construction: the half-swap
    (read PUT->GET role) x the parity flip = identity on absolute cycles. The
    DMC spans shift one position — the Y=4 lever — with no second driver left
    to over-grow.
* **`dmc_dma_defer_load_entry`** (nes-core): the pre-cycle while-gate INVERTS to
  `put_cycle` under the flag (pre-cycle reads are flip-invariant in VALUE, but
  with no start-flip the pre value now IS the upcoming access label, and the
  DMC noop half is the PUT half).
* **nes-apu (3 reconciliations, all references-ordering — no knobs):**
  1. **TriCNES `DMCDMADelay` placement** (the load-delay countdown put-branch,
     Emulator.cs:1217): the `$4015`-enable LOAD delay becomes `2` put end-ticks
     of `dmc_tick_end` (write-cycle's own end counts when put — "really like
     2:3"), replacing the every-cycle countdown whose `apu_phase ? 4 : 3`
     initial value baked in the floor GET parity. Verified TriCNES-exact:
     write-on-put -> halt from W+3, GET at W+5; write-on-get -> halt from W+4,
     GET at W+6 (both match the TriCNES trace semantics). The
     `implicit_stop_edge` -1 (a CPU-cycle-unit calibration of the every-cycle
     countdown) is skipped under the gate (TriCNES has no such adjustment).
     **This closed the Implicit `$500` +1-iteration shift** (the first
     measurement had KEY1's `01,01` at idx[11,12]; with the put-end placement
     it is at idx[10,11] == KEY1).
  2. **`complete_dmc_dma` off-phase suppress basis**: the
     `dmc_reload_suppress_outputs = 1` guard's `!apu_phase` term means "the GET
     landed on the off-phase half". The normal GET half is `apu_phase`-true at
     floor but `apu_phase`-false under the end-flip, so the term inverts —
     without this the floor-dead suppress path fired at EVERY collapse GET in
     the 1-byte-loop contexts, producing the `$540` `2C` (=44) cascade runaway.
     **This removed the `$540` runaway.**
  3. The implicit-abort `cycles_until_output` at the load GET is **wall-exact
     WITHOUT adjustment** (measured, then proven: the shifted GET reads
     `apu_phase` flipped (-1) but the byte-timer pre-end-tick (+1) — the two
     cancel; Implicit `$500 == KEY1` confirms). An attempted +1/-1
     re-expression moved the abort 2 iterations early and was REVERTED (noted
     in-code).

The landed Implicit stack (halt-subpos / abort-cancel / abort-timer-phase /
reenable-phase / load-get-entry) stays composed. The `cannot_run = 2` set-site
rides `complete_dmc_dma` inside the engine's GET branch. `dmc_abort_is_get_cycle`
(pre-cycle read) is flip-invariant in value and becomes TriCNES-exact in
meaning — no change needed.

## The residual map — ONE structural surface: delayed `$4015` DMC status

The three remaining sweep deltas under the flag all route through the same
missing mechanism, TriCNES `APU_DelayedDMC4015` (Emulator.cs:9547+):

* TriCNES applies EVERY `$4015` DMC-status write with a delay
  (`put ? 3 : 4` half-ticks, decremented every cycle in `_EmulateAPU`); our
  `write_status` applies `dmc.set_enabled()` IMMEDIATELY at the write access.
* **Explicit idx[7] (`00` vs KEY `04`)**: TriCNES's explicit don't-abort edge —
  when the disable write coincides with "the APU cycle that fires a DMC DMA"
  (`(timer==2 && get) || (timer==rate && put)`), the disable delay is EXTENDED
  to `put ? 5 : 6` so the just-armed reload DMA runs to completion = the KEY's
  non-monotonic `04` recovery. Our immediate `set_enabled(false)` zeroes
  `bytes_remaining` -> `needs_dma` false -> no arm -> `00`.
* **Implicit `$520` (KEY2's single `01` at idx[10])** and **`$540` (plateau 2
  early + `03,03` lead-in)**: the Loop2/Loop3 re-enable races read the
  immediate status where hardware sees it 2-3 cycles late; the abort/reload
  chain alignment against the JSR pushes (Loop2) and the measurement window
  (Loop3) is off by exactly that application delay. (At floor these passed via
  compensating calibrations — the every-cycle load countdown's
  `implicit_stop_edge` -1 and the halt-subpos boundary pre-arm — which are
  expressions of the same missing delay in floor units.)

Porting the delayed-status mechanism is a deep DMC-core/APU-register surface
(it interacts with `$4015` reads, the IRQ flag, and likely the Delta-Mod [18] /
APU-Reg-Activation [6] residuals, which sit on the same register-application
timing). It is the named next structural step, NOT a parity/scheduling knob —
the DO-NOT-RETRY tables remain binding and nothing here re-tries them.
