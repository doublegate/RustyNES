# RA-4: ship the C1 win (R1 substrate, flag-gated); the 4-test DMA tail is deferred — full traced rationale

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Branch:** `feat/v2.0-from-main`
**Decision:** ship the master-clock R1 substrate (`mc-r1-substrate` + `mc-r1-dmc-idle-halt` +
`mc-r1-apu-unified-clock`) as the validated, **flag-gated** C1-accuracy axis (AccuracyCoin RAM
**92.09%**, the C1 IRQ trio `cpu_interrupts_v2` #1-#4 **closed**, DMA cluster **6/10**, no wedge); the 4
remaining DMA-tail tests are `#[ignore]`'d under R1 as the documented architectural residual. Default
build stays **94.24%** lockstep, byte-identical. Floor `5ceab83`+`a8e482e`.

## What ships

- **Default (no features):** unchanged v1.7.0 lockstep behavior — AccuracyCoin **94.24%**, the DMA
  cluster (incl. the 4 tail tests) **passes**, the C1 IRQ trio **fails** (the known v1.0 residual).
- **R1 substrate (opt-in):** `mc-r1-substrate,mc-r1-dmc-idle-halt,mc-r1-apu-unified-clock` —
  AccuracyCoin **92.09%**, the **C1 IRQ trio closes** (`cpu_interrupts_v2` #1-#4: `cli_latency`,
  `nmi_and_brk`, `nmi_and_irq`, `irq_and_dma`), DMA cluster **6/10**, no DMASync wedge. The 4 DMA-tail
  tests (`DMC DMA + OAM DMA` $0477, `Explicit DMA Abort` $0479, `Implicit DMA Abort` $0478, `DMA + $4015
  Read` $045D) **fail** under R1 — the deferred residual.

The two are a genuine, independent trade (see BP below): default's `$2002` phase passes the DMA tail and
fails C1; R1's (more hardware-faithful) `$2002` phase passes C1; the DMA tail is a *separate* DMC-DMA
issue, not a `$2002` trade.

## RA-1 (banked win): the DMC byte-timer is now clocked at cycle START, unified with the APU

`mc-r1-apu-unified-clock` moves the DMC byte-timer + arms from the end-of-cycle `tick_dmc` (the F-2 split:
gated `!put_cycle`, frozen-arm during span) into `tick_with_external` at cycle START on `apu_phase` — the
default/pulse/noise path — matching Mesen's `ProcessCpuClock` at `StartCpuCycle`. The DMC now advances
*through* its own DMA span (arms self-guard on `!pending_dmc_dma`, no cascade). **Make-or-break gate
passed: no wedge** (where advancing the DMC during its span re-wedged under the *decoupled* model).
Behavior-preserving vs the prior R1 floor: 92.09%, C1 intact, 6/10, nes-apu green. This is a real
structural improvement banked from the investigation.

## Why the 4-test DMA tail is deferred (the traced root cause)

The tail's failing tests all hinge on `CheckDMATiming` Y: a `JSR CheckDMATiming; CPY #4; BNE FAIL` gate
that requires the measured DMC-DMA duration == 4. Under R1, Y = **3** (the DMC DMA measures one cycle too
short). The full investigation eliminated every candidate:

1. **P2 (burst-DMA rewrite misdirected).** The R1-vs-default divergence *source* is the boot `$2002`
   vblank-poll loop (`8045: LDA $2002 / BPL`), not the DMA — cold-boot census: zero divergence at
   `$4014`/DMC/interrupt vectors.
2. **V-0 (`$2002` timing is hardware-correct).** Per nesdev `PPU_frame_timing`, R1 samples `$2002` at
   read-start (`master_clock − ppuOffset`) = the reference alignment. The cold-boot master-clock
   alignment is **inert for Y** across all 24 sub-cycle values (washes out at the per-frame vblank
   re-sync + DMASync re-pin).
3. **RA-0/1/2 (Mesen-faithful DMC model still gives Y=3).** Matched Mesen's clock placement (RA-1),
   span structure (RA-2 halt+dummy+align+get via `dmc_need_halt`/`dmc_need_dummy_read`), and GET parity
   (even = `!put_cycle`, confirmed by the Mesen oracle: all 2171 GETs even-parity). The span *structure*
   is GET-landing-equivalent; flipping the GET parity **wedges**.
4. **CM-1 (apu_phase flip wedges).** nesdev confirms RustyNES's APU-rate DMC timer is hardware-correct
   (period 428 = 214 APU cycles); the offset is the absolute `apu_phase` alignment, and flipping it
   wedges (the DMC fire misaligns from the CPU's `$4000` read).
5. **WD-1 (CPU-rate DMC timer inert + regresses).** Mesen clocks the DMC timer at CPU rate (period 428),
   RustyNES at APU rate (213); but the period is *even*, so CPU-rate clocking still expires on the same
   parity (inert for Y) and regresses the battery (DMC IRQ/output-cycle timing shift).
6. **BP (the `$2002` phase and the DMA tail are INDEPENDENT).** Swept the PPU-sample offset −4..+4
   measuring Y + the C1 trio together: the offset *is* wired (C1 `nmi_and_brk` flips PASS↔F01, passes
   only at delta 0 and 4) but the **DMA tail is inert (Y=3 at every delta)**. DMASync re-pins the
   CPU↔DMC at the test, isolating the tail from the `$2002`/CPU-PPU phase. **So C1 and the tail are not
   one axis** — sacrificing C1 gains nothing on the tail.
7. **Wedge trace (the binding constraint).** Under a parity shift the DMASync `LDA $4000;BNE` spin reads
   `$4000`=`$40` forever; the DMC fires at its sample addr (`$EF79`) while the CPU reads `$4000`
   **unhalted** — the DMC misses the `$4000` read, the conflict never lands. The halt *must* land on the
   `$4000` read for the conflict, and that read's parity yields span 3.
8. **Trailing-stall (both variants wedge).** Holding the CPU halted one extra cycle after the GET
   (duration 3→4, GET landing fixed): the re-read variant clobbers the conflict `$00`; the pure
   time-burn variant wedges early (adding a cycle to every DMC DMA shifts the CPU stream and breaks
   DMASync's fixed clockslide timing).
9. **Architecture (no interleaving difference).** Mesen's `MemoryRead` (`NesCpu.cpp:491`) is
   `ProcessPendingDma(addr); StartCpuCycle; Read; EndCpuCycle` — all DMA cycles *before* the read,
   **structurally identical to `read1`**. There is no "interleaved" rewrite that differs from what we
   have; the duration 3-vs-4 is the halt-start parity (which read gets halted), which is wedge-locked.

**Conclusion:** the tail requires the DMC DMA to halt the `$4000` read (conflict) *and* span 4
simultaneously; in RustyNES these are 1 cycle apart and every shift either wedges (DMC misses `$4000`)
or breaks DMASync/the 6/10. The blocker is the cumulative CPU-cycle parity reaching that `$4000` read,
which is not the `$2002` phase (BP), not the cold-boot alignment (V-0), and not CPU instruction timing
(nestest zero-diff). Closing it would require a different cumulative-cycle model with no identified,
non-wedging lever — deferred. Ten-plus independent levers refuted across P2/V-0/RA/CM/WD/BP/wedge-trace.
