## THE 1-CYCLE IS THE CalcDMADuration CATCH PHASE, NOT THE DMA

CalcDMADuration measures Y by sliding a `LDA $4000` read 1 cycle/iteration (loop=575,
DMA period=576) until the DMA HALTS AT the $4000 read — then the held address is
$4000, the GET drives $00 on the open bus, and the post-GET resume `LDA $4000` returns
that $00 (BEQ taken, Y = iteration count). The $00 is open-bus-visible for exactly 1
cycle (the next resume read overwrites it — confirmed: 49030473 sets open bus=$DA), so
the catch requires the DMA-halt to land precisely on the $4000-read cycle.

In the traced iteration the $4000 read lands at GET+6 (49030478, reads $40, misses) —
so this is NOT the catch iteration; the slide must bring the DMA-halt onto the read.
Y=3 (RustyNES) vs Y=4 (TriCNES) means RustyNES's DMA-halt aligns with the $4000 read
ONE iteration earlier. That alignment is the byte-timer-fire-vs-CPU-$4000-read phase,
SET UPSTREAM by the DMASync_50CyclesRemaining exit (`JSR DMASync` $00-detect + 350
clockslide + RTS = "DMA exactly 50 cycles after RTS"). If RustyNES's DMASync $00-detect
lands 1 cycle off, the whole "50 cycles after RTS" anchor — and thus the CalcDMADuration
catch iteration — is off by 1.
