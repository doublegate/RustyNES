RustyNES is a cycle-accurate Nintendo Entertainment System emulator written in pure Rust. It targets the accuracy bar set by Mesen2, higan, and ares: tight lockstep scheduling at PPU-dot resolution, sub-instruction PPU events visible to subsequent CPU code, and a non-linear audio mixer with band-limited synthesis. The same deterministic core runs on desktop, in the browser, on RetroArch, and here natively on iPhone and iPad.

ACCURACY FIRST
- The emulation core scores 100% on the AccuracyCoin hardware-accuracy suite.
- Passes the blargg, kevtris, and MMC3 test-ROM batteries.
- 172 mapper families, so a huge library of titles just works.
- Deterministic by design: the same input always produces the same result.

BUILT FOR iOS AND iPadOS
- A native SwiftUI app with a Metal renderer and ProMotion pacing.
- On-screen multi-touch controls plus MFi / hardware controllers for up to four players.
- Save states and battery-backed cartridge saves.
- Rewind and fast-forward.
- CRT, scanline, and NTSC-composite video filters.
- iCloud save-state and settings sync across your devices.
- Game Center, Lua scripting, and RetroAchievements support.
- Local and online (room-code) netplay.
- Full-featured on iPhone and iPad, with multitasking and Stage Manager on iPad.
- VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, and colorblind palettes; English and Spanish.

BRING YOUR OWN GAMES
RustyNES ships with no copyrighted game data. Load your own legally obtained .nes / NES 2.0 ROM files from Files, iCloud Drive, or the share sheet — they stay on your device.

OPEN SOURCE
RustyNES is free and open source (MIT OR Apache-2.0). Source, issue tracker, and documentation are on GitHub at github.com/doublegate/RustyNES.

This is a release-candidate build distributed through TestFlight while we validate on-device stability ahead of the public App Store launch.
